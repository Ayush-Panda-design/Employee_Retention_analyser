Feature Engineering

5 derived features were constructed to capture richer signals beyond raw columns.

MonthlyIncome / (TotalWorkingYears + 1)
IncomePerExperience
Normalises salary against career length. Low values → underpaid relative to experience → higher attrition risk.
MonthlyIncome / JobLevel
IncomePerLevel
Detects pay inequity relative to seniority. Low ratio signals misaligned compensation for job level.
YearsAtCompany / (TotalWorkingYears + 1)
CompanyLoyaltyRatio
Measures what fraction of a career was spent at this company. High ratio → loyal employee.
YearsSinceLastPromotion / (YearsAtCompany + 1)
PromotionRate
Stagnation signal. High ratio means no promotion despite long tenure — a known attrition trigger.
pd.cut(YearsAtCompany, [0,2,5,10,40])
TenureGroup
Bins tenure into 4 cohorts (0–2, 2–5, 5–10, 10+). Early cohorts show significantly higher exit rates.

Top Attrition Drivers

Key findings from exploratory data analysis on the IBM HR dataset.

01
Overtime
Employees working overtime are 3× more likely to leave
02
Low Monthly Income
Below-average compensation is the strongest continuous predictor
03
Younger Age (18–30)
Younger employees leave more frequently, especially in first 2 years
04
Research & Development Dept
Highest departmental churn rate despite competitive salaries
05
Frequent Business Travel
Correlates with poor work-life balance and higher attrition
06
Low Job Satisfaction
Rating of 1 or 2 doubles the probability of leaving
